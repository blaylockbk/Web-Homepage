What is the HRRR Wake Finder?

The HRRR Wake Finder is a tool for boaters who are looking for the smooth, glass-like water-- perfect conditions for water skiing and wake boarding.

Lake chop is generally caused by:

  1. Wind Speed
  2. Wind Duration
  3. Fetch - distance wind travels over water

Therefore, boaters who are looking for glass-like boating contitions are looking for areas of the lake that are the lest windy.

The HRRR Wake Finder makes forecasts from the High Resolution Rapid Refresh (HRRR) weather model available for boaters so they can know ahead of time which areas of the lake may be less windy, giving them the advantage over other boaters.

As an example, this chart illustrates the relationshiop between wave height, wind speed, wind duration, and fetch length. The chart is more useful for boaters on the ocean where large fetches, higher wind speeds, and wave height is greater, but you get the idea.

When the wind speed is high, when winds last a long time, and when the fetch is long, the water surface becomes more choppy as waves develop. These waves are caused by kinetic energy being transfered from the atmosphere to the water surface.

How NASA uses the wind speed/wave height relationshiop

I imagine the relationshiop between wave height and wind speed has been well known by sailors and boaters for some time now. In the modern science era, NASA has used this relationship to derive wind surface wind speeds over the ocean from space. In 1999 NASA launched the QuickSCAT satellite, which used an active remote sensing technique for measuring wind speeds. It sent down burst of microwave energy to earth which signal bounced off the surface and returned to the satellite. This beam of energy is similar to a weather radar, but the frequency of this signal was designed to penetrate clouds and precipitation. Ocean waters with larger wave heights would scatter more of the signal and reduce the amount returned to the satellite. The amount of signal reflected back to space could then be transformed to a wind speed and wind direction over the oceans. It was a polar orbiting satellite with a 1,800 km swath allowing it to observe nearly the entire earth in a single day. This instrament was increadibly valuable for weather forecasting (especially hurricane forecasting) and mariners. When the satatellie mechanics failed in 2009, NASA rapidly built a replacement.(See the Wikipedia article)

The RapidScat instrament was installed on the International Space Station in 2014 Below is an image of wind speed derived from the RapidScat instrament on January 28, 2015 when a strong winter storm was impacting the north east.

What is the HRRR

HRRR is a high resolution weather model that is updated every hour. Its purpose is to aid in short term weather forecasting on a regional level by increasing model resolution (HRRR resolution is 3km whereas the NAM is at 12 km and the GFS at 13km) and using additional data assimulation techniques including assimulating radar retrevals. That said, increased model resolution doesn't gaurantee better wind forecasts. Utah Lake is only 5 or 6 model grid points across. Please understand that it these models are flawed and actual weather conditions may be different.

Every hour I do the following:

  1. Get HRRR Forecast
  2. Process raw HRRR and produce visualization

How to read the charts

If you are unfamilar with reading weather charts, here are some terms you should know.

  • Model: A simulation of weather conditions made by a computer for a certain area and time.
  • Valid Time: The time the weather model forecasted conditions for.
  • Initilization Time: The time the computer started its weather simulation.
  • Wind Barb: A image of a flag that indicateds the wind speed and direction.
