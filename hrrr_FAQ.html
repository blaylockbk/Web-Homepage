Who is archiving the HRRR data?

The HRRR archive at the University of Utah is managed by the MesoWest group. As a courtesy, please contact Brian Blaylock when you download data (we would like to know how often and how many people are grabbing data).

While you can find current HRRR output on the NOMADS server, there is no official HRRR output archive. To our knowledge, this is the only HRRR archive.

What is the HRRR archive?

The HRRR archive is a collection of output from NCEP's High Resolution Rapid Refresh model. This is a model developed by NOAA ESRL and is run operationally every hour at NCEP.

The HRRR creates a forecast with 3-km grid spacing for 18 hours over the continental United States.

Output files contained in the archive include:

  • Surface Fields in grib2 format ~75 MB (e.g. hrrr.t00z.wrfsfcf00.grib2). Forecast hours are available after August 1, 2016
  • Pressure Fields in grib2 format ~315 MB (e.g. hrrr.t00z.wrfprcf00.grib2). Forecast are not included in this archive.
  • Bufr Soundings for Salt Lake City, Provo, and Ogden Utah. (I believe Penn State archives HRRR bufr soundings. Check with them if you need other sites.)
  • Processed HRRR files (e.g.2016080300F01hrrr.grib2) used my Jim Steenburgh to display wind and precipitation variables on weather.utah.edu
  • Experimental HRRR-Alaska : Begining Summer 2016??
  • Experimental HRRR (HRRRv3) surface analysis: After December 1, 2016

Unfortunately we don't download the .idx files. I think you can create your own with wgrib2.

Where is the University of Utah HRRR archive?

Our archive of HRRR data older than August 2016 is compressed and unavailable at the moment. We are in the process of exploring other archiving methods that would make it easier to access and keep things organized. Please understand, this is an expensive project and providing a public archive of HRRR data isn't exactly our top priority. We sure wish NOAA would provide an archive through NOMADS like they do for the RAP and other models.

The archive is located on the MesoWest servers in two places. The URL you're looking for depends on your period of interest.

  • April 18, 2015 -- December 31, 2015 go here: https://api.mesowest.utah.edu/archive/__archive5__/.
  • January 1, 2016 -- present go here: https://api.mesowest.utah.edu/archive/. HRRR only available AFTER September 01, 2016

Navigate to the directory named by the date you are interested in (i.e. 20160923), then models, then hrrr.

For example: https://api.mesowest.utah.edu/archive/20160901/models/hrrr/

(Obviously there is a lot more stuff in our archive space than just HRRR output.)

How do I get the HRRR data if what I'm looking for is compressed?

When we first started downloading the HRRR, we didn't realize it would be so popular. The archive wasn't built in a way to effectively distribute the data to you. Because we have so much stuff in our archive, we have to compressed most of the days. The most recent month or two should be uncompressed, but if you find what you're looking for is compressed, follow the following instructions to get what you need:

  1. Go to the archive and navigate to the YYYYMMDD you are interested in. For example, https://api.mesowest.utah.edu/archive/20160101/.

What version of HRRR is in this archive?

Prior to August 23, 2016 we downloaded the operational HRRR from this URL: http://nomads.ncep.noaa.gov/pub/data/nccf/nonoperational/com/hrrr/prod/. This was HRRRv1. Although the URL says "nonoperational", it was the operational HRRR. The files just hadn't been moved to the operational space. (In order to get the nonoperational versions we would have had to downloaded from a password protected URL in the ESRL web space.)

Some days and hours in our achive may not be available. Either because the forecast wasn't run, or our servers were not able to download the file quick enough (sometimes the download page is very slow).

HRRRv2 was implemented at NCEP on August 23, 2016 for the 12z run. That day I began downloading from the new URL http://nomads.ncep.noaa.gov/pub/data/nccf/com/hrrr/prod/.

For more details check out the following announcement: http://www.nws.noaa.gov/om/notification/tin16-26rap_hrrrraaa.htm

What days have HRRR data available?

We began downloading HRRR anaylsis for each hours on April 18, 2015 and have continued to download since then.

Data for 2015 are stored here, and data for 2016 are stored here.

Prior to July 27, 2016 only the analysis hours for the pressure and surface fields were downloaded from the NOMADS server.

On July 27, 2016 I began to download the surface fields for each forecast hour. Unfortunately, the pressure fields are large files (~315 MB per hour) and we have decided not to archive the forecasted pressure fields.

Tables describing the HRRR data availability for each month can be found here: http://home.chpc.utah.edu/~u0553130/available_HRRR_archive/

(I haven't created these tables for compressed months yet)

In summary:

  • 2016-07-27: started grabbing 15 hour sfc forecasts
  • 2016-08-24: started grabbing 18 hour sfc forecasts
  • Missing data on 2016-09-09 (lots)
  • Missing data on 2016-09-25 (hour 06, f15)
  • 2016-12-01: started grabbing experimental HRRR surface analysis
  • 2016-Summer??: started grabbing HRRR Alaska

Why do you archive the HRRR?

The HRRR archive is used for various research purposes.

  1. Initialize Weather Research and Forecast (WRF) model with HRRR analyses as initial and boundary conditions. (Requires HRRR pressure fields). More info here.

  2. Compare HRRR analyses and forecasts to observed conditions. Specifically for areas near large wild fires. More info here.

Who uses this archive?

The initial purpose of this HRRR archive was to serve atmospheric science research at the University of Utah, mostly used by graduate students. For example, I used it to initialize WRF simulations.

Since the archive has been published on-line I have received inquiries and download requests from National Weather Service employees, a researcher at the National Institute of Standards and Technology, a United State Air Force Captain, a commercial wind power company, university professors, graduate students, and post docs.

If this archive is useful to you, contact the most important person you know at NCEP and request they develop an official HRRR archive. Thank you.
