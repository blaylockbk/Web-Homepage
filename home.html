PhD Candidate


I'm a Utah native from Spanish Fork, Utah and I'm currently pursuing a PhD in atmospheric science at the University of Utah. My advisor is John Horel.

I've been a part of the Mountain Meteorology Research Group since November 2012 and have worked for MesoWest and several field campaigns (SARP, MATERHORN, UBOS, GSLSO3S, SONGNEX).

My experience includes WRF modeling, meteorological instruments and observation, Python, data analysis, and web programming. My current research interest involves improving the Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) model for weather and air quality predictability in Utah and verifying HRRR analyses and forecasts near wild fires.

Upon completion of my degree I hope to expand my skills and begin a lifelong career in meteorological model development (specifically the WRF/HRRR models and whatever comes next) and data assimilation. NASA SPoRT, NOAA ESRL and IMSG all seem like good places to work. While there seems to be many deficiencies in the current United States numerical weather prediction capabilities, I want to be a part of the solution. Hurricanes are not my focus, but I'd love to one day fly with the hurricane hunters on a dropsonde mission.
